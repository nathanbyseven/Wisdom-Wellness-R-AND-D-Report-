01 / The Original Brief

What We Were
Asked to Build

When Wisdom & Wellness first approached Sevenstudios, the brief was broad and, as we later discovered, partially aspirational. Below is the original ask — and what it was missing.

  • A fully designed and developed iOS and Android application
  • Integration with Ghutte and GoKollab
  • Support for membership, events and ticketing, programs and journeys, podcasts, booking and resources
  • A seamless experience where external tools feel embedded
  • A future‑proof platform that can evolve over time

The brief described an outcome — not a technical scope. What Wisdom & Wellness wanted was a beautiful, unified home for their ecosystem. What was missing was clarity on how the existing platforms work, who owns what, and what building on top of them actually means in practice. This is not a criticism — it is simply the reality of most early‑stage digital product briefs.


Because the brief left major technical questions unanswered — particularly around Ghutte and GoKollab — we proposed three options and worked through Phase Zero to uncover the full picture before committing to a scope. This document is the result of that process.

03 / Technology Stack

What We
Found Out

The Back-End Engine
Ghutte
Ghutte is not an independent platform. It is a white‑labelled, licensed implementation of GoHighLevel — managed by ClubSheIs under their own branding.

W&W do not own the underlying system. W&W are a tenant on a licensed platform. It handles CRM, automated messaging, membership, subscriptions, and payment processing via Paystack.
The Front-End Interface
GoKollab
GoKollab is the mobile‑facing, community interface layer of the GoHighLevel ecosystem — the current "app environment" for W&W members.

It is not designed to be embedded into a third‑party native app. Attempting this creates architecture conflicts and significant App Store risk.
The Licensing Agency
ClubSheIs
ClubSheIs are a sales and marketing systems agency who came on board in December 2025. They license GoHighLevel and provide it as Ghutte.

Their strengths are revenue automation, sales funnels, email marketing, and GoKollab deployment. They are not a product design or UX agency.
Known Technical Limitations
Payment Sync Failures
Paystack does not communicate cleanly with Ghutte. Members who fail to pay are not always automatically removed — subscriptions can lapse without triggering correct automations. This is an active revenue leakage.
Ownership vs. Licensing Conflict
Everything W&W runs on is licensed — not owned. GoHighLevel owns the platform. ClubSheIs licenses it. W&W are the end client. If terms, pricing, or policies change, W&W have limited recourse.
GoHighLevel Integration Depth
GoHighLevel provides REST APIs and webhooks for data-level sync, but these are not designed for turning GoHighLevel into a transparent backend for a separately branded native app. This is not a standard or low-risk pattern.
GoKollab Cannot Be Embedded
GoKollab is the GoHighLevel community interface. It is not designed to be extracted and placed inside a third-party native app. Attempting to wrap it creates dual-system complexity and potential App Store compliance issues.
App Store Compliance Risk
Apple has strict requirements around apps that aggregate or re-skin third-party systems. Building a shell that wraps GoHighLevel/GoKollab creates a risk of rejection or later flagging during App Store review.
Very Recent Migration
W&W migrated from Circle to GoKollab in December 2025 — weeks before this engagement began. The system is still settling. Layering a new app on top of a recent migration adds further risk.

06 / Option C Assessment

Is Option C
Still Viable?

Build a standalone iOS & Android app shell
Fully viable. A WW app itself can be built cleanly and independently when the timing is right.
✓ Viable
Integrate GoKollab "inside" the app
GoKollab is the GoHighLevel community interface. It is not designed to be embedded into a third-party native app. Doing so creates architecture conflicts and significant App Store risk.
✕ Not Viable
Use Ghutte as the primary app backend
GoHighLevel exposes APIs and webhooks, but not in a way that safely turns it into a first-class backend for a separate custom app. Deeper integration would be non-standard and likely brittle.
⚠ Risky / Limited
Modular architecture for future scale
Fully viable. This is good practice regardless of timing. The architectural principles remain valuable for any future build.
✓ Viable
Phase 1 launch in 2026
W&W themselves have indicated they are not ready for a development investment of this scale in 2026. The financial and operational conditions are not aligned for a full build this year.
⏸ Paused

Option C as originally scoped should not be executed as a deep integration/orchestration layer on top of Ghutte/GoKollab right now, because those platforms are not designed to be integrated in the way the original proposal assumed. This is not a failure of the idea — it is Phase Zero doing its job. The core principles of Option C remain strong, but the timing and implementation strategy need to change.

08 / Open Questions

W&W's Questions
Answered Directly

Do we need an app right now?
+
Not immediately. Your revenue goals for 2026 are better served by fixing your current platform (payment sync, membership automation) and growing the community on the system you already have. An app is a medium-term accelerator, not an immediate necessity.
Can we build an app that integrates with Ghutte / GoHighLevel?
+
In a surface-level way, yes. You can build an app that links to GoHighLevel-powered pages via secure WebViews and deep links, and sync data via APIs and webhooks. As a deep, primary backend for a separate app — it is not advisable. GoHighLevel's integration surface is not designed to safely power a fully independent native app, and attempting that would create more problems than it solves — especially if W&W ultimately wants to own its own backend anyway.
Can we have the GoKollab experience inside our own app?
+
No — not without rebuilding it. GoKollab is the GoHighLevel community interface. It cannot simply be lifted and placed inside a separate app. If you want that kind of experience in your own app, it needs to be designed and built from scratch in a future ownership phase.
What is ClubSheIs building vs. what is Sevenstudios building?
+
ClubSheIs are building and maintaining your revenue and systems engine — the Ghutte configuration, sales funnels, automations, membership management and branded GoKollab app. Sevenstudios are your product and brand partner — responsible for your product strategy, UX, design system and eventual custom app architecture. These are not the same thing — but they need to be coordinated by W&W so they serve the same vision.
Is the R997K investment still on the table?
+
The full Option C scope as originally proposed is not the right next step given current conditions. A restructured, phased engagement — starting with Phase A (strategy, research, brand work) and, when appropriate, evolving into a clearly defined MVP build — is the appropriate path. Sevenstudios is prepared to reframe the investment structure accordingly.
What happens to all the Phase Zero work?
+
It is preserved and packaged. This document is the output. The ecosystem map, user-flow drafts, technical findings and product brief are living assets that remain valid and become the foundation for Phase B when W&W is ready. Nothing is wasted.
